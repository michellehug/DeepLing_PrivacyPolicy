Privacy Policy – DeepLing (Alpha Test)
Last updated: January 2026

DeepLing is an alpha-stage language learning application. This privacy policy explains how information is handled during testing.

Information We Collect
DeepLing does not collect personal data.
There are no user accounts, no logins, no email collection, and no identity data.
The app only plays audio; it does not record or store microphone input.
All learning content and progress data is stored locally on the user’s device.

Third-Party Services
DeepLing uses Firebase solely to facilitate API calls (Gemini AI services).
No personal data is transmitted to Firebase for user identification or tracking purposes.

Analytics and Tracking
No analytics services are used.
No tracking, profiling, or behavioral monitoring is performed.

Advertising
DeepLing does not show ads.

Data Sharing
No data is sold, shared, or transferred to third parties.

Children’s Privacy
DeepLing is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from children.

Legal Basis (GDPR – EU)
Because no personal data is collected or processed, GDPR personal data obligations do not apply to user data processing.

CCPA (US – California)
DeepLing does not collect, sell, or share personal information as defined by the CCPA.

Data Storage
All app data remains on the user’s device during the alpha test.

User Rights
Because no personal data is collected or stored, there is no personal data to access, export, or delete.

Changes
This policy may be updated as the app evolves beyond alpha testing.
